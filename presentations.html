Presentations

MIT Museum Girls Day Presentation

November, 2017

The MIT Museum hosts a Girls Day twice a year. In November 2017, I was invited to give a talk about physics I have done as an undergraduate.

I gave my presentation on the Optical Trapping experiment I completed in MIT's Junior Laboratory class. Since this event was aimed at girls of age 10 and above, I spent the majority of the talk explaining the physics of using LASERs to trap objects, and how such an experimental set-up can be used to measure the force of an onion cell's motor protein.

Junior Laboratory (8.13) Presentation

May, 2017

Junior Laboratory (8.13) is an experimental physics class offered to upperclassmen in the MIT Physics Department. During the class, students collect data, give oral presentations, and write techincal manuscripts for four famous or fundamental experiments. Every student also delivers a public oral presentation at the end of the semester. I chose to present on the Optical Trapping experiment, a biophysics experiment which uses a powerful LASER to measure the force of the myosin protein in onion cells.
